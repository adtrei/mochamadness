🔥 Biggest Story: Iowa Topples Defending Champion Florida

9-seed Iowa hit a go-ahead 3-pointer with 4.5 seconds left to stun 1-seed Florida 73–72 in Tampa, knocking the defending national champions out in the Round of 32.

It was the upset of the tournament so far, and only the ninth time since 1979 that a 9-seed has beaten a 1-seed. Iowa reaching the Sweet 16 wasn’t in most brackets’ plans, so the damage spread broadly across the field.

⚠️ Injury Watch: Joshua Jefferson (Iowa State)

Iowa State’s All-American forward sprained his ankle in the first round and watched the Kentucky game in a walking boot. The Cyclones still rolled 82–63 behind Tamin Lipsey’s 26 points and 10 assists, but Jefferson’s availability for the Sweet 16 is a major question.

That matters a lot for Ken and Max B2, who both need Iowa State to stay alive as their champion pick.

🔑 Key Matchups for the Pool

Michigan vs Alabama (Friday)
Three brackets — Colby, Kevin, and Adam #1 — need Michigan to keep rolling. Michigan looked dominant in the first weekend, beating Howard 101–80 and Saint Louis 95–72. Alabama is dangerous, but Michigan enters as the favorite.

Duke vs St. John’s (Friday)
Two champion picks — Zoe and Owen — ride on Duke, and several other brackets need the Blue Devils in the Elite 8. Duke survived a first-round scare against Siena, then rolled TCU 81–58. St. John’s is very real after knocking off Kansas.

Iowa State vs Tennessee (Friday)
Max B2 and Ken both need Iowa State to survive, likely without Jefferson at full strength. Tennessee upset Virginia and has been playing top-10 defense for the past six weeks. This may be the most dramatic game of the round.

Houston vs Illinois (Thursday)
Two champion picks — Max B3 and Adam #3 — need Houston here. Houston has steamrolled opponents so far (78–47 over Idaho, 88–57 over Texas A&M) and gets the Sweet 16 at the Toyota Center in Houston — a very real home-court edge.

Arizona vs Arkansas (Thursday)
Three brackets — Max B1, Tyler, and Mary — are hanging their hopes on Arizona. The Wildcats have looked sharp (92–58 over LIU, 78–66 over Utah State), but Arkansas is hot after winning the SEC tournament and extending its winning streak to six.

Michigan State vs UConn (Friday)
A pure pedigree showdown. Dov needs Michigan State. Ashley needs UConn. UConn won back-to-back titles in 2023 and 2024, and Dan Hurley’s group is chasing a third championship in four years. Michigan State has been one of the best defensive teams in the country.

Purdue vs Texas (Thursday)
Natalie and Lambs need Purdue to stop Texas’s Cinderella run. Texas came through the First Four, upset BYU, and knocked off 3-seed Gonzaga to reach the Sweet 16 as an 11-seed.

📈 Scenarios to Watch

  • If chalk holds across the Sweet 16: the top of the leaderboard stays jammed, and the Elite 8 becomes a total bloodbath.
  • If Michigan falls: three brackets lose their champion, wiping 32 points from each ceiling and blowing up the Michigan-heavy middle of the pack.
  • If Iowa State falls: two champion picks go down, while current leaders like Max B3 (Houston) and Colby (Michigan) gain breathing room.

The $252 first prize is absolutely still up for grabs.
